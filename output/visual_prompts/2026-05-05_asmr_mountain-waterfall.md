## Creative Brief

The viewer sits at the edge of a mountain waterfall pool, deep inside an ancient temperate forest. It is the first twenty minutes of morning — that narrow window when pre-dawn light begins filtering through the canopy and the mist above the water is still thick and unhurried. Wet mossy granite and broad fern fronds fill the foreground, still heavy with overnight damp, grounding the viewer in the physical space. The waterfall anchors the mid-ground: its motion is constant, irregular, and inherently soothing. Volumetric god-rays enter from the upper-left, catching the fine spray and turning it into drifting light. The palette is cool — deep greens, blue-grey mist, slate rock — punctuated only by warm amber where the light breaks through. There is no destination and no task; the viewer simply sits and breathes with the water. The cinemagraph loop works because the waterfall's cascade is perpetual and non-repeating, the mist has no clear beginning or end, and the completely static camera gives the eye nothing to chase — only stillness interrupted by water.
