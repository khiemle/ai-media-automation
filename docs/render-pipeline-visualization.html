Long-Form Render Pipeline

Interactive visualization of how a 3-hour 4K render is built — chunks, audio layers, music loop, blackout, and the seams that used to glitch. Companion to docs/render-pipeline.md.

1 · Orchestrator flow

User clicks "render" → service routes to render_youtube_chunked_orchestrator_task. The orchestrator plans N chunks of 300 s each, persists them to render_parts, and dispatches a Celery chord: parallel chunk tasks with the concat task as the callback.

2 · The full audio is rendered ONCE, then muxed

This is the v1.2.1 architectural fix. Each video chunk is encoded -an (video only). The full audio mix — music + all sound layers — is encoded in one continuous ffmpeg pass after every chunk completes. Then a stream-copy mux glues them together.

4 · Music playlist — seamless loop crossfade v1.2.3

Most music files don't naturally loop. aloop repeats the buffer byte-for-byte, so the last sample butts up against the first sample — a click at every loop boundary. For a 5 min track repeated to 3 hours, that's a click every 5 min. Broadband / ambient music masks it; tonal music exposes it.

before v1.2.3 hard aloop boundary

Single track of 300 s, looped to 10800 s. 35 hard cuts → 35 audible clicks for tonal music.

musicaloop -1

↑ yellow lines mark loop boundaries where samples don't match

after v1.2.3 N-copy acrossfade

Same 300 s track replicated as 37 ffmpeg inputs. 36 pairwise acrossfade=1.5 filters blend every adjacent pair — within iterations and across loop boundaries.

musicacrossfade

↑ green pulses are crossfades — no audible discontinuity

5 · Sound layer scheduling

The three scheduled layers (midground, foreground, random_sfx) use the same schedule_sfx_layer(pool, interval_min, interval_max, seed, start_s, end_s) helper with different seed offsets so the layers don't collide on the same RNG stream. The schedule is deterministic — same inputs always produce the same timestamps.

Layer config

layer interval seed offset events / 3h (avg) typical use
background looped continuously (no schedule) — 1 input × stream_loop -1 rain, stream, white noise
midground 15–30 s 0 ~480 leaves, ambient noises
foreground 30–60 s 100003 ~240 birds, distant animals
random_sfx 60–180 s 200003 ~90 rare events (thunder, owl)

before v1.2.3 aloop hard music loop

v1.2.1 fixed the chunk seams. But the music itself still hit a hard loop boundary every M seconds (track length). Same "weird sound every 5 min" symptom returned for users whose music tracks were ~5 min long.

v1.2.3 N-copy acrossfade music loop

Replicate the playlist as N ffmpeg inputs, crossfade every consecutive pair (within and across iteration boundaries), atrim to target. No hard loop boundary anywhere in the music WAV.

7 · Blackout overlay

video.black_from_seconds tells the renderer to fade to black at that absolute time. Common for "X hours then sleep" videos. Per-chunk logic:

  • Chunk entirely before blackout: normal visual + audio, no overlay.
  • Chunk straddling the blackout point: visual + audio + a fading-in color=c=black overlay starting at the local timestamp.
  • Chunk entirely after blackout: visual input replaced by lavfi color=c=black directly — skips expensive video decode for chunks that would just be solid black anyway.
visualw/ blackout @ 2h15m

Chunks 0–26: rendered normally · Chunk 27: fades to black mid-chunk · Chunks 28–35: lavfi black source, no visual decode

8 · Resume / cancel

render_parts as the source of truth

Every chunk's state lives in youtube_videos.render_parts (JSONB). Each entry has idx, start_s, end_s, status, file_path, task_id, timestamps. Atomic jsonb_set updates so parallel chunk workers don't race.

cancel

cancel_chunked_render revokes every chunk's task_id plus the concat callback's task_id. This is why the orchestrator persists task_ids to render_parts BEFORE dispatching the chord.

resume

resume_chunked_render resets failed/running entries to pending and re-runs the orchestrator. The orchestrator preserves completed entries — but ONLY if file_path ends in .video.mp4 (the v1.2.2 legacy invalidator). Legacy chunk.mp4 entries get re-rendered.
